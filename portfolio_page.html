Overview

Project Summary

Progressive Insurance's Robinson Strategy targets growth in Multiproduct Households (MPHH) — customers who hold two or more Progressive products. MPHH customers retain at significantly higher rates and generate 40–60% more lifetime premium than single-product customers. This project answers the central operational question: which households should receive cross-sell outreach, in what order, and what is it worth to get it right?

Starting from 150,000 synthetic agency households snapshotted over two years, I built a complete analytics pipeline — from data generation and SQL segmentation through propensity modeling and financial impact simulation — and delivered two production-ready Streamlit dashboards: a cross-sell propensity explorer for campaign teams and a benchmarking tracker for Robinson Strategy executive reporting. Every number in the project traces back to a verified computation.

The analysis surfaces that property quote abandonment is the single highest-ROI intervention target, that the optimal outreach window is 1–3 contacts per household per year, and that the portfolio has been behind its Robinson growth target in 6 of the last 8 quarters — with the gap widening. These findings are directly actionable by sales leadership, agency operations managers, and BI/control partners.

Analysis

Key Findings

Finding 01
Quote abandonment is the highest-ROI intervention target
Households that started a property quote but did not complete it convert at 38.3% — nearly 1.5x the 26.1% baseline. The 25,922 abandoned quotes in the dataset represent the single most recoverable population for any outreach campaign.
1.5× baseline conversion rate
Finding 02
Independent Agent channel leads at 29.7% MPHH rate
The Independent Agent channel converts 8 percentage points above Direct Online (21.8%), confirming that relationship-driven selling is the dominant cross-sell mechanism. Channel mix has a larger impact on MPHH rate than any single demographic attribute.
+7.9pp vs. Direct Online
Finding 03
Outreach sweet spot is 1–3 contacts per year
Conversion rate climbs from the zero-contact baseline through three contacts, then plateaus or reverses. Households receiving 4+ contacts show no incremental gain — representing wasted outreach budget that could be reallocated to untouched high-propensity households.
Peak conversion: 2–3 contact band
Finding 04
Robinson Strategy is behind target in 6 of 8 quarters
The portfolio has trended below the Robinson growth target since 2023 Q3, with the gap reaching –2.85pp by 2024 Q4. Q1–Q2 showed year-over-year improvement; Q3–Q4 reversed — pointing to a mid-year outreach program slowdown as the likely driver.
–2.85pp gap in 2024 Q4
Finding 05
24–60 month tenure cohort is the conversion sweet spot
Established customers in the 24–60 month tenure window convert at the highest rates across all acquisition cohorts. Very new households (under 6 months) convert poorly; the 60+ month cohort shows slight decline, suggesting long-tenure habituation to single-product status.
Peak: 24–35 month tenure bucket
Finding 06
Elite + Independent Agent households convert at 38%+
The highest-value cross-sell segment — Elite tier homeowners acquired through Independent Agents — converts at over 38% with an average projected CLTV above $1,800. At approximately 8,400 unconverted households, this segment represents a concentrated, high-return intervention target.
Avg CLTV $1,840 per household

Approach

Methodology

01
Synthetic Dataset Design
Generated 150,000 households using a logistic function with domain-calibrated coefficients to produce realistic conversion patterns — not random noise. Property quote initiation and outreach contact frequency were explicitly wired as the two strongest behavioral signals, matching known P&C insurance cross-sell dynamics. Seed=42, fully reproducible.
02
SQL Analysis (12 Queries, 5 Sections)
Wrote ANSI SQL (PostgreSQL / Snowflake compatible) covering data quality validation, segmentation analysis, financial impact quantification, cohort and behavioral analysis, and model support queries. All queries use CTEs and window functions. The final query produces the intervention candidate list used by the dashboard's At-Risk Explorer.
03
Propensity Modeling with Leakage Controls
Trained a Gradient Boosting classifier on 8 features. Propensity score, propensity tier, and projected CLTV were explicitly excluded from the feature set and documented in the data dictionary as leakage-prone. In production, the propensity score is the model output — not an input. Evaluated using AUC-ROC, lift by decile, and cumulative gain curves.
04
Financial Impact Framework
CLTV = (anchor premium + estimated property premium) × projected tenure years × 18% margin factor. The revenue simulator models campaign ROI across save rate scenarios (5%–40%) and cost-per-contact inputs ($10–$150). At 15% save rate and $35 per contact, the program generates an 11× ROI against the full unconverted population.
05
Dual Streamlit Dashboards
Delivered two single-file Streamlit applications sharing the same dataset and color system. The propensity dashboard (6 tabs) covers exploration, drivers, model risk, financial impact, cross-industry translation, and recommendations. The benchmarking dashboard (6 tabs) covers the Robinson Strategy tracker, YoY growth, cohort retention heatmaps, agent leaderboards, state performance, and pipeline health.
